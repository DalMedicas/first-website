SOCRÁTES TRACJETORY

Sócrates Brasileiro Sampaio de Souza Vieira de Oliveira, commonly known as Sócrates, was a Brazilian footballer whose journey left an indelible mark on the sport. Born on February 19, 1954, in Belém, Brazil, Sócrates was not only a gifted player but also a highly intelligent and charismatic figure, earning him the nickname "The Doctor" due to his medical degree

Sócrates rose to prominence in the 1970s and 1980s, showcasing his exceptional skills as an attacking midfielder. He possessed a unique blend of elegance, vision, and technical prowess, making him a standout performer on the field. His career reached its peak during his time with Corinthians, one of Brazil's most beloved football clubs

What set Sócrates apart was not only his footballing ability but also his deep intellect and principled approach to the game. Off the field, he was known for his outspoken views on politics and social issues, embodying the belief that football could be a platform for broader societal discussions

A key part of Brazil's national team during the 1982 and 1986 FIFA World Cups, Sócrates played a crucial role in orchestrating the team's attacking play. His leadership qualities were further evident when he captained the national side. Despite Brazil's ultimate disappointment in those tournaments, Sócrates' impact was lasting, and his style of play influenced future generations of footballers

Sócrates' footballing journey extended beyond Brazil, with stints in Italian football, notably with Fiorentina. His elegant style and flair on the ball endeared him to fans across the globe. Despite battling injuries during various phases of his career, Sócrates remained a revered and influential figure

Off the pitch, Sócrates was a man of principles. He played a pivotal role in the Corinthians Democracy movement, advocating for player involvement in club decisions. His commitment to social causes and his advocacy for democratic values set him apart as a footballer with a social conscience.
