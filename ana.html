ANA MOSER TRACJETORY

Ana Moser, a Brazilian volleyball legend, has left an indelible mark on the sport with her remarkable career and contributions. Born on August 14, 1968, in São Paulo, Brazil, Ana discovered her passion for volleyball at an early age. As she grew up, her talent and dedication became evident, propelling her into the world of professional volleyball

Ana Moser's journey in the sport began in the late 1980s when she joined the Brazilian national team. Her outstanding skills as an outside hitter quickly earned her recognition, and she became a key player for the national squad. Moser played a crucial role in Brazil's success in international competitions, including the Olympic Games

One of the pinnacle moments in Ana Moser's career occurred during the 1992 Barcelona Olympics. She played an instrumental role in leading the Brazilian women's volleyball team to a historic bronze medal, marking a significant achievement for Brazilian volleyball on the global stage

Apart from her success on the national team, Ana Moser also made her mark in club volleyball. She played for top clubs in Brazil and internationally, showcasing her prowess and contributing to numerous titles and championships. Her versatility, athleticism, and leadership qualities made her a valuable asset to any team she played for

Off the court, Ana Moser has been actively involved in promoting sports and education. She founded the Instituto Esporte & Educação (Institute of Sport and Education), a non-profit organization aimed at using sports as a tool for social development and education. Through her work, Moser has impacted the lives of many young individuals, instilling values of teamwork, discipline, and resilience.
