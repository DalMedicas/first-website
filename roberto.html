ROBERTO TRACJETORY

Roberto Carlos, born on April 10, 1973, in Garça, São Paulo, Brazil, is widely regarded as one of the greatest left-backs in the history of football. His journey from a small town in Brazil to becoming a global football icon is a testament to his extraordinary talent, powerful free kicks, and enduring success on the pitch

Carlos began his professional career with the Brazilian club Palmeiras in 1991, showcasing his exceptional speed, defensive skills, and overlapping runs from the left-back position. His performances quickly drew attention, and he earned a move to Europe, signing with Inter Milan in 1995. Although his time in Italy was relatively brief, Carlos continued to impress with his dynamic style of play

However, it was during his stint with Real Madrid, which began in 1996, that Roberto Carlos truly solidified his status as a football legend. His partnership with the Galácticos, including Zinedine Zidane, Luis Figo, and Ronaldo, led to unprecedented success for the club. Carlos played a crucial role in Real Madrid's three consecutive UEFA Champions League victories from 1998 to 2002, etching his name in football history

Renowned for his thunderous free kicks, Roberto Carlos scored some of the most memorable goals in football, including the iconic bending free-kick against France in 1997. His ability to contribute offensively from the left-back position revolutionized the role, making him a trailblazer for attacking full-backs in the modern game

Carlos's illustrious career also saw him represent the Brazilian national team with distinction. He played a key role in Brazil's triumph in the 2002 FIFA World Cup, contributing both defensively and offensively to the team's success

Beyond his playing career, Roberto Carlos transitioned into coaching and administrative roles within football. His impact on the sport extends to mentoring and inspiring the next generation of players.
