BRUNINHO TRACJETORY

Bruninho, whose full name is Bruno Mossa de Rezende, stands as a prominent figure in the world of volleyball, known for his exceptional skills, leadership, and significant contributions to the sport. Born on July 2, 1986, in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Bruninho hails from a family deeply rooted in volleyball, with his father, Bernardinho, being a renowned coach and former player

Bruninho's journey in volleyball began at a young age, shaped by his exposure to the sport through his family. As he honed his skills, it became evident that he inherited both the talent and the passion that defined his father's legacy. His journey into professional volleyball accelerated when he joined the youth ranks of renowned Brazilian club Flamengo

In 2006, Bruninho made his debut with the Brazilian national team, marking the beginning of an illustrious international career. His role as a setter, combined with his tactical acumen and court vision, quickly established him as a pivotal player. Bruninho played a vital part in Brazil's dominance on the global volleyball stage, contributing to numerous victories and championships

One of the highlights of Bruninho's career came during the 2016 Rio Olympics. As the captain of the Brazilian men's volleyball team, he led them to a historic gold medal on home soil, solidifying his status as one of the premier setters in the world. His strategic playmaking and leadership qualities were instrumental in Brazil's success

Beyond the national team, Bruninho has played for various clubs, both in Brazil and abroad, leaving an indelible mark with his performances. His versatility, precise sets, and ability to adapt to different playing styles have made him a sought-after asset for any team he joins.
