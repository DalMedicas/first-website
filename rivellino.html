RIVELLINO TRACJETORY

Roberto Rivellino, born on January 1, 1946, in São Paulo, Brazil, is celebrated as one of the most elegant and skillful midfielders in the history of football. His journey from the streets of São Paulo to the global football stage is a testament to his exceptional talent and creative prowess on the field

Rivellino's footballing prowess became evident at an early age, and he joined the youth ranks of Corinthians, one of Brazil's iconic football clubs. His dazzling dribbling, precise passing, and powerful long-range shots quickly set him apart as a rising star in Brazilian football

In 1965, Rivellino made his professional debut with Corinthians, and it didn't take long for him to become a focal point of the team's success. His performances at the club level earned him a spot in the Brazilian national team, where he would go on to make an indelible mark

Rivellino played a pivotal role in Brazil's triumph in the 1970 FIFA World Cup held in Mexico. His mesmerizing displays of skill, particularly his trademark "Elastico" move, captured the imagination of football fans worldwide. Brazil's attacking prowess, led by Rivellino, Pelé, and Jairzinho, showcased a brand of football that remains legendary

Known for his left foot's finesse and vision on the field, Rivellino had an illustrious career that included stints with top clubs such as Fluminense and Al-Hilal in Saudi Arabia. His time at Fluminense further solidified his status as a football icon, contributing to the club's success in domestic competitions

Rivellino's legacy extends beyond his playing career. He became a football ambassador, continuing to influence the sport through coaching and various roles within the football community. His impact on the game earned him recognition as one of the greatest midfielders in the history of Brazilian football.
